Built for the people conversation

How I develop, level, and manage the team.

People-first isn't a slogan — it's a method. Here's how I know if someone's getting it, how I grow them, and how I handle a rep who isn't.

How I know someone's "getting it"

I read leading indicators, not vibes: discovery volume, multithreading per deal, qualification depth using MEDDIC (Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Identify pain, Champion), and whether their forecast holds. A rep who is getting it can walk me through where each deal will actually stall and who they still need in the room — without me prompting.

Leveling & growth

Each rep gets a named path: Account Executive I → Account Executive II → Major Account Executive, with the book shifting from commercial to enterprise as they earn it. I run a bench, not bodies — a manager-in-training track so the bar holds as the team scales from 5 toward 8.

Performance management, done right

For an under-performing rep: a documented 30-day leading-indicator plan with weekly inspection, built with People & Culture from day one. The coaching is real and the timeline is real. People stay because the standard is fair and clear — not because it's soft.

The one-on-one I actually run

I don't take the deal. I ask the question that makes the rep find the gap themselves:

"Walk me through your last three deals — where did each one actually stall, and who did you still need in the room?" The rep names the gap. They leave owning one concrete commitment we inspect together next week.

Early-detection lens: I'm not just managing this quarter's number. I'm watching for the rep who is starting to disengage — and intervening while it's still a coaching conversation.

The operating system, end to end

The weekly rhythm that makes pipeline predictable.

Structure in a fast-moving environment — proactive, not reactive. Hybrid, in office Monday and Wednesday, low travel. Every loop below feeds the forecast and the roadmap.

Monday — Pipeline standup

In office. Commit movement and priorities for the week. Everyone knows their gap-to-number before Tuesday.

Tuesday — Deal-coaching one-on-ones

Teach, don't fish. We work the MEDDIC qualification gaps (Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Identify pain, Champion) and the next best action. The rep owns the call; I coach the thinking.

Wednesday — Product Business Team sync

In office. Push live customer and competitive intel into the roadmap — direct product input is a real differentiator here.

Thursday — Forecast & commit lock

Waterfall dashboard. Commit versus best case versus gap. Anything without a confirmed next step and an economic buyer moves out of commit.

Friday — Scorecard review

Leading indicators, ramp, bottom-quartile plans, skip-levels. The same inspection cadence that lifted Sales-Qualified Leads per rep 22% year over year.

Always-on — Gamify

Leaderboards on outbound and pipeline generation, seasonal incentives, recognition. Momentum is a system, not a mood.

The big-picture view

Why this is the moment for Swagger.

Mid-last-year through the first quarter was slow — budget uncertainty, and artificial-intelligence (AI) token spend took priority. The second quarter is ramping back. The macro tailwind and the product are lining up, and a first-line team that sells on business impact is how SmartBear captures double-digit growth in Application Programming Interface (API) and test management.

The "vibe-coding" crisis

AI-assisted code ships roughly 2.7× more security vulnerabilities. As teams generate more code faster, API governance, design standards, and contract testing stop being nice-to-haves — they're risk controls. That is Swagger and Pactflow's core value.

Regulated buyers are spending

Fidelity expanded, the U.S. Army asked for an on-premises option, and a German government tax agency deployed. Financial services, healthcare, and government need governance and self-hosted options — and they buy on compliance, not hype. Precision outbound lives here.

The return case is provable

The Forrester Total Economic Impact study puts SwaggerHub at 227% Return on Investment (ROI). When reps lead with quantified business impact instead of features, the justification writes itself — and that is the qualification standard I install.
